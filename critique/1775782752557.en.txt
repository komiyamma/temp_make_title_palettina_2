1. Introduction
This painting depicts a riverside landscape viewed from beneath an old stone arch bridge. The inner wall of the arch forms a framework that guides the gaze from the dark shade toward the sunlit world outside. Thick brushstrokes cover the canvas, effectively conveying the hardness of stone, the softness of leaves, and the water's clarity.

2. Description
The texture of the arched bridge's stones is expressed three-dimensionally using thick paint. The river flowing into the foreground is highly transparent, allowing pebbles on the shallow riverbed to be clearly seen. Lush green plants on the right add organic vitality. Beyond the arch, the water surface shines brightly under the sunlight, accompanied by dense trees.

3. Analysis
The most striking feature is the bold impasto technique. The joints of the masonry, individual leaves, and ripples are composed of thick, distinct brushmarks. The contrast between the deep browns in the shaded foreground and the vibrant greens in the sunlit background is highly effective. This calculated contrast emphasizes the framing effect and creates spatial depth.

4. Interpretation and Evaluation
This artwork transforms a casual landscape into a dramatic visual experience through the materiality of paint and dynamic lighting. The composition of viewing a dazzling world from a dark shelter can be seen as a psychological metaphor for hope. The expressive power that conveys energy through rough brushwork is magnificent, and the painter's color sense is highly evaluated.

5. Conclusion
Through rich brushmarks and vivid light contrast, this painting poetically depicts the scenery under a bridge. Despite being an accumulation of rough touches, excellent harmony and realistic presence are maintained throughout. It is an attractive work that beautifully encapsulates the vivid confrontation between nature and man-made structures.
