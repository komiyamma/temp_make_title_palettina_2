1. Introduction
This painting is a powerful still life depicting a traditional Japanese purification fountain. The moment water flows from a dragon-shaped spout is captured using heavy oil techniques. This work is a creative piece that fuses traditional Japanese design with the movement of water through an expression full of materiality.

2. Description
A large stone dragon head is positioned on the left. Clear water overflows from its mouth and is poured into a wooden ladle in the foreground. In the background, a violent flow of water like a waterfall is drawn in white, while surrounding rocks are summarized in deep colors. The textures of the scales and ladle are recreated three-dimensionally by applying thick layers of paint.

3. Analysis
The diagonal line from the dragon's head to the ladle guides the eye and creates dynamic momentum. The green suggesting moss, the gray of the stone, and the ocher of the ladle harmonize to create an antique atmosphere. Specifically, the white of the water and the golden highlights of the dragon's eyes are effectively placed to emphasize a mysterious vitality.

4. Interpretation and Evaluation
This work can be interpreted as embodying spirituality, such as the purification of water in a sanctuary. The painter's descriptive power is high, successfully coexisting the conflicting textures of hard stone and fluid water. The boldness of the composition and the richness of overlapping color planes speak of the artist's advanced sense of form and technical maturity.

5. Conclusion
What initially felt like a realistic painting of a dragon sculpture reveals an expressionistic intent to depict the intersection of light and matter upon closer inspection. It is a masterpiece imbued with deep spirituality where tradition and innovation coexist.
