1. Introduction
This evocative oil painting depicts the iconic Stockholm City Hall at the transition from dusk to twilight. The composition is anchored by the prominent brick tower, whose summit is crowned with a gleaming golden lantern and the Three Crowns. A Swedish flag flutters in the upper right, while the distant city skyline is a tapestry of warm, twinkling lights. Viewed from an elevated balcony, the scene is a masterful study of light and atmosphere, capturing the poetic beauty of the Swedish capital as it transitions into night.

2. Description
The central red-brick tower rises majestically against a cool, periwinkle sky, its surface catching the last remnants of daylight and the glow of interior lamps. To the right, a large Swedish flag is rendered with broad, expressive strokes, its blue and yellow colors standing out against the darkening atmosphere. Below the tower, the water of the harbor reflects the brilliant orange lights of the city and the soft colors of the sky. In the far background, additional buildings and a church spire are visible, their forms softened by the evening haze and the myriad of artificial lights.

3. Analysis
The artist employs a striking contrast between the warm, fiery oranges of the brickwork and city lights and the cool, desaturated blues of the sky and water. This complementary color scheme creates a vibrant visual tension that brings the scene to life. The brushwork is energetic and textured, with thick applications of paint highlighting the golden lantern and the reflections on the water's surface. Perspective is effectively achieved through the inclusion of a weathered stone balcony in the foreground, which establishes a clear sense of depth and invites the viewer into the scene from a specific vantage point.

4. Interpretation and Evaluation
The work successfully conveys a sense of civic majesty and the enchanting quality of urban life after sunset. By framing the tower with the flag and the foreground balcony, the artist emphasizes the significance of the architecture as a cultural and historical landmark. The use of light is particularly sophisticated, suggesting not just the physical illumination of the city, but also a more symbolic, "inner" radiance of the community. Technically, the painting demonstrates a strong grasp of color theory and a confident, expressive handling of the oil medium, resulting in a cohesive and emotionally resonant image.

5. Conclusion
Through its vibrant execution and thoughtful composition, this painting offers a compelling vision of Stockholm's unique atmosphere and architectural heritage. The interplay between the solid, historical brickwork and the transient, glowing lights of the modern city provides a rich visual narrative. Ultimately, the work invites the viewer to experience the tranquil and celebrated beauty of the city at one of its most evocative moments. It is a masterful example of how atmospheric landscape painting can elevate a well-known urban subject into a profound and lasting sensory experience.
