1. Introduction
This composition presents a tranquil still life arrangement featuring glass bottles on a wooden surface. The visual creation uses a watercolor aesthetic to depict botanical elements within the clear vessels. A warm atmosphere permeates the scene through careful lighting and composition.

2. Description
Four glass bottles with cork stoppers form the central focus. They contain various substances, including amber fluid and green foliage. Dried floral elements are positioned in the foreground on the rustic wooden table. The background remains soft and blurred, suggesting a sunlit window.

3. Analysis
The maker utilizes soft rendering to establish a gentle transition between objects and their surroundings. Golden illumination filters through the background, casting warm highlights on the glass surfaces. Textural contrasts are evident between the smooth glass, rough corks, and delicate dried flowers. The spatial arrangement guides the gaze from the foreground blooms toward the clustered vessels.

4. Interpretation and Evaluation
The arrangement evokes historical apothecary traditions and botanical studies. The luminous amber tones and organic materials suggest a connection to natural remedies. The execution demonstrates a refined understanding of light, effectively capturing the sunlit glass. This tranquil presentation successfully fosters a contemplative mood.

5. Conclusion
This botanical still life provides a captivating study of light, texture, and organic forms. The harmonious integration of clear vessels with dried flora creates a cohesive arrangement. Students can observe how illumination and material contrasts elevate simple objects into a compelling visual narrative.
