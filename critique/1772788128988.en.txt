1. Introduction
This visual work presents a still life composition featuring a guitar, a wine bottle, a glass, and sheet music. The subjects are rendered through a fragmented, geometric approach that deconstructs their traditional forms. The composition utilizes a contrasting palette of warm and cool tones.

2. Description
An acoustic guitar occupies the central area, depicted with interlocking angular planes. To the right, a dark wine bottle stands alongside a partially filled glass of red liquid. These objects rest upon scattered pages of sheet music. The background consists of overlapping triangular and diamond facets that merge with the central subjects, creating a flattened perspective.

3. Analysis
The structure relies heavily on diagonal lines and intersecting shapes, breaking down objects into multiple viewpoints simultaneously. Warm hues of orange, yellow, and brown dominate the guitar and foreground elements. These warm colors contrast with the cool blues, grays, and deep blacks present in the background and the bottle. Light and shadow are applied to individual facets rather than originating from a single source, enhancing the overall geometric texture.

4. Interpretation and Evaluation
The fragmented style operates as a shattered mirror reflecting rhythm and sound. By dismantling familiar objects, the composition invites viewers to examine the fundamental shapes and colors that constitute reality. The interplay between the recognizable still life elements and the abstract background creates a dynamic tension. This tension maintains visual interest while challenging conventional perceptions of space.

5. Conclusion
The piece successfully merges musical motifs with a fractured visual language. Through its geometric deconstruction and contrasting palette, it offers a complex reexamination of traditional still life subjects. This balance of form and color provides a structured yet rhythmic visual experience.
