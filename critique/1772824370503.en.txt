1. Introduction
This composition presents a close-up still life featuring a glass filled with ice alongside vibrant red strawberries. The objects are arranged closely together, drawing focus to their distinct textures against a heavily textured, earth-toned background. The pigment application utilizes thick, visible strokes to build up the physical forms.

2. Description
On the left side, a clear glass contains irregular, angular blocks of ice that reflect white and yellow light. To the right, a cluster of ripe strawberries rests against the background, topped with small green leaves. The fruit is rendered with short, overlapping marks of brilliant red and orange.

3. Analysis
The visual structure relies on the striking contrast between the cool, transparent glass and the warm, opaque strawberries. Lighting originating from the upper left illuminates the textured fruit while casting bright reflections across the ice. The dark brown and ochre background marks push the brightly colored subjects forward.

4. Interpretation and Evaluation
The arrangement pairs the suggested coldness of the ice with the natural sweetness associated with the fruit. This juxtaposition creates a tactile tension between the sharp edges of the ice and the organic forms of the strawberries. The energetic application of color transforms everyday objects into a dynamic visual experience.

5. Conclusion
The piece successfully captures the interplay of light across contrasting materials using a highly expressive technique. A careful orchestration of warm and cool tones brings a lively energy to the otherwise static subjects. The intense focus on surface qualities rewards close observation and elevates a simple arrangement into a compelling study.
