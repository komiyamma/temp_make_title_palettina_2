1. Introduction
This composition presents a detailed still life featuring glass vessels displayed on an old wooden surface. The visual creation focuses on dried botanical elements and small bottles containing varied liquids. A quiet, nostalgic atmosphere is created through the intentional use of soft lighting and selective focus.

2. Description
Four miniature glass bottles with cork stoppers stand together in the center. They are filled with an assortment of materials, including dark amber liquid and pale dried leaves. Scattered dried flowers surround the bases of the bottles on the weathered wood. The background is rendered as a soft blur, ensuring the central objects remain the primary focal point.

3. Analysis
The maker uses a narrow depth of field to separate the intricate central subjects from the hazy background. Gentle illumination brings out subtle reflections on the glass and emphasizes the delicate structure of the dried plants. Textural contrasts highlight the difference between smooth glass, rough cork, and weathered timber. The arrangement effectively guides the viewer from scattered foreground elements to the clustered vessels.

4. Interpretation and Evaluation
The arrangement suggests a connection to historical herbalism and the careful preservation of natural ingredients. Muted, warm tones and organic subjects evoke feelings of timelessness and traditional remedies. The execution shows a solid command of lighting and texture, bringing out the inherent beauty of simple materials. This carefully composed scene successfully instills a sense of calm reflection.

5. Conclusion
This rustic still life provides a valuable demonstration of texture and selective focus in visual composition. The balanced grouping of glass bottles and dried flowers results in a cohesive display. Students can observe how deliberate lighting choices elevate common items into a compelling subject.
