1. Introduction
This landscape painting features hydrangeas, symbolic of the rainy season, rendered with the soft light of a wet day. The gradations of pale blue and white that envelop the scene, along with the depiction of rain dripping from the leaves, provide the viewer with a sense of cool, quiet emotion. A key characteristic of this work is its treatment of rain not merely as background but as an active element that brings movement and moisture to the entire canvas. Based on keen observation, this piece successfully visualizes even the humid atmosphere, possessing both a high level of painterly completion and deep spirituality.

2. Description
Large hydrangea blooms, shifting from white to pale blue, are arranged across the center and foreground. Each individual floret is delicately painted, with fine rain droplets clinging to their surfaces. The large leaves supporting the flowers are deep green, their wet textures rendered realistically through the depiction of water streaks. From the upper-right, thin lines represent falling rain, while the background trees are blurred, adding a fantastical sense of depth. The entire composition is unified by a subdued color palette, illuminated by the soft, diffused light typical of a rainy day.

3. Analysis
The composition creates a visual rhythm through the contrast between the round forms of the hydrangeas and the vertical lines of the falling rain. In terms of color, the cool tones of the flowers harmonize with the green of the leaves, effectively recreating the calm atmosphere of a rainy day. Technically, the fine lines capturing falling drops and the depictions of water dripping from the leaves introduce a sense of temporal flow into the scene. While the brushwork is meticulous for the subjects, the intentional omission of detail in the background successfully highlights the presence of the hydrangeas.

5. Conclusion
