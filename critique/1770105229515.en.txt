The subject is a dark bay or black horse, its coat glistening with a healthy sheen under a strong light source. 3. Analysis
Technically, the painting is notable for its vigorous impasto and expressive handling of paint. The artist uses thick, oily strokes to build up the muscular structure of the head and the coarse texture of the mane. Lighting is used strategically to define the contours of the snout, the curve of the jaw, and the intricate details of the leather straps. The color palette is dominated by rich, earthy umbers and siennas, punctuated by the brilliant metallic gold and the single, bold stroke of red from the tassel.

Initially, the viewer is struck by the bold textures and dramatic lighting, but it is the sensitivity of the horse窶冱 gaze that leaves a lasting impression.
